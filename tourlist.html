Everest base Camp

Everest Base Camp (EBC) is one of the most iconic trekking destinations in the world, situated at an elevation of approximately 5,364 meters (17,598 feet) in the heart of the Himalayas. This base camp serves as the starting point for mountaineers attempting to summit the world's highest peak, Mount Everest (8,848 meters or 29,029 feet).

Annapurna base Camp

Annapurna Base Camp (ABC) is a renowned trekking destination situated in the heart of the Annapurna Himalayas in Nepal. Nestled at an altitude of about 4,130 meters (13,550 feet), the base camp offers panoramic views of some of the highest peaks in the Annapurna Range, making it a popular trekking choice for adventurers seeking a diverse range of landscapes and cultural experiences.

Langtang base Camp

Langtang Base Camp is a captivating trekking destination situated in the Langtang Valley of Nepal, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural experiences, and stunning mountain vistas. Nestled within the Langtang National Park, this base camp stands at an elevation of approximately 4,380 meters (14,370 feet) and provides a spectacular view of the surrounding peaks, including Langtang Lirung.

Kathmandu

Kathmandu, Nepal is a captivating destination that seamlessly blends culinary delights, cultural gems, and a rich historical tapestry. In this vibrant city, the culinary scene is a sensory adventure, offering local favorites like dal bhat and momo. Thamel, with its lively atmosphere, hosts a variety of eateries showcasing a fusion of traditional Nepali and international flavors. The narrow alleyways hide street food stalls, introducing visitors to the creative wonders of sel roti and yomari.

Pokhara

Pokhara, Nepal, is a serene and picturesque city that invites visitors into a realm of natural beauty, adventure, and cultural richness. Nestled beside Phewa Lake and surrounded by the Annapurna Range, Pokhara offers a tranquil escape. Culinary experiences in the city are diverse, with lakeside cafes serving local delights like dal bhat and momo, providing a perfect blend of flavors against the backdrop of the stunning Himalayas.

Chitwan

Chitwan, located in the southern part of Nepal, offers a unique blend of wildlife, nature, and cultural experiences. Known for its lush national parks, the city is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. The culinary scene in Chitwan reflects the region's diverse influences, with local eateries offering a mix of traditional Nepali flavors and international cuisine.

Phoksundo

Phoksundo, or Phoksundo Lake, is a mesmerizing alpine lake nestled in the Dolpa district of northwestern Nepal. Situated within the Shey Phoksundo National Park, this high-altitude freshwater lake is one of the deepest in the country, with crystal-clear turquoise waters that reflect the surrounding snow-capped peaks.

Pathivara

Pathivara Devi Temple, also known as Pathibhara Temple, is a sacred Hindu pilgrimage site located in the Taplejung district of eastern Nepal. Situated atop a hill at an altitude of about 3,794 meters, the temple is dedicated to Goddess Pathivara, considered a manifestation of Goddess Durga.

Ghalegaun

Ghale Gaun is a picturesque village nestled in the Lamjung District of Nepal. Perched at an altitude of around 2,100 meters, this traditional Gurung village offers a serene escape into the lap of the Himalayas. The village is renowned for its warm hospitality, cultural richness, and stunning mountain views, making it a hidden gem for those seeking an authentic Himalayan experience.
